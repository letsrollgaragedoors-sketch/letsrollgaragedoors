7 Warning Signs Your Garage Door Spring Is About to Break

June 2, 2026 Let's Roll Team 5 min read

A broken torsion spring is one of the most common, and most dangerous, garage door failures. When a spring snaps, your door can become completely inoperable in seconds, leaving your vehicle trapped or your home unsecured. The good news: springs usually give you warning signs before they fail entirely.

Never attempt to repair or replace garage door springs yourself. Torsion springs are under extreme tension and can cause serious injury. Always call a certified technician.

A torsion spring system showing the gap from a broken spring. Never attempt repairs yourself, always call a certified technician.

1. The Door Feels Unusually Heavy

Springs counterbalance the weight of your garage door. If a spring is weakening or partially broken, the door will feel much heavier than normal when you try to open it manually. A balanced door should lift easily with one hand, if it takes real effort, that's a red flag.

2. You Hear a Loud Bang

When a torsion spring snaps, it makes a sound like a gunshot or loud bang, often described as someone throwing something heavy in the garage. If you hear this sound and your door won't open, your spring has likely broken.

3. There's a Visible Gap in the Spring

Visible gaps in a torsion spring coil indicate a broken spring that needs immediate replacement.

Look at the springs above your garage door. A broken torsion spring will have a visible gap, typically a separation of 2 inches or more in the coil. If you see this, don't use the door until it's repaired.

4. The Door Opens Crooked

If one spring fails in a two-spring system, the door will rise unevenly, one side will be higher than the other. This puts enormous strain on the cables, tracks, and opener, and can cause secondary damage quickly.

5. The Opener Strains or Fails

Your garage door opener is designed to work with a properly functioning spring system. If the opener is straining, reversing immediately, or triggering its safety stop when trying to open the door, a failing spring is often the cause.

6. Cables Are Loose or Hanging

Broken springs cause the cables to go slack or hang freely on the sides of the door. Loose cables are both a symptom and a danger, they can snap or tangle, causing additional damage.

7. The Spring Looks Stretched or Worn

Over time, springs lose tension and may appear stretched, elongated, or corroded. If the coils look widely spaced or the spring has visible rust, it's nearing the end of its lifespan and should be replaced proactively.

How Long Do Garage Door Springs Last?

Standard torsion springs are rated for approximately 10,000 cycles. For an average household opening the door 4 times a day, that's roughly 7 years. High-cycle springs rated for 25,000-50,000 cycles are available and worth the investment.

One of the most common questions we get is: "How much will this cost?" Garage door repair pricing varies depending on the specific issue, parts needed, and urgency. Here's an honest, up-to-date breakdown for Austin homeowners in 2026.

Common Repair Costs in Austin

Spring Replacement: $150-$350

This is the most common repair. Torsion spring replacement typically runs $150-$250 for a single spring and $200-$350 for a two-spring system. Always replace both springs at the same time, if one breaks, the other is usually near the end of its life too.

Opener Repair: $100-$250

Opener issues range from circuit board failures to motor replacements. Minor repairs like sensor alignment or remote reprogramming cost $75-$125. Full motor replacement runs $150-$250. A brand-new opener installation ranges from $250-$500 installed.

Cable Replacement: $100-$200

Cables typically come in pairs and should be replaced together. The job takes about an hour and includes the parts and labor to re-tension the spring system.

New Door Installation: $800-$2,500+

A basic single-car steel door installed runs $800-$1,200. Premium materials (wood, glass, carriage-house styles) and double-car widths push costs to $1,500-$2,500 or more.

Roller & Hinge Replacement: $75-$150

Worn rollers and hinges cause noise and wear. Replacing them is a quick, affordable fix that dramatically improves door performance and reduces strain on the opener.

Emergency Service Fees

Most companies charge an after-hours or weekend service call fee of $50-$100. At Let's Roll, we're transparent about this upfront and work to keep emergency costs reasonable.

When to Repair vs. Replace

If your door is over 15-20 years old and repairs would cost more than 50% of a new door's value, replacement is often the smarter investment. Our techs will always give you an honest recommendation.

5 Easy Garage Door Maintenance Tips That Add Years to Its Life

May 15, 2026 Let's Roll Team 3 min read

Your garage door is one of the hardest-working mechanical systems in your home, often opening and closing 1,500+ times per year. A little preventative maintenance goes a long way. Here are five things you can do in under 30 minutes to extend its life and avoid expensive repairs.

1. Lubricate the Moving Parts Every 6 Months

Use a white lithium grease or silicone-based lubricant (not WD-40) on springs, hinges, rollers, and the opener chain or screw. Avoid lubricating the tracks, they should stay clean and dry for proper roller contact.

Apply lubricant to hinges, springs, and rollers, never to the tracks themselves.

2. Test the Balance Monthly

Disconnect the opener (pull the red cord) and manually lift the door halfway. It should stay in place. If it falls or shoots up, the springs are out of balance and need professional adjustment.

3. Inspect and Replace Weather Stripping

Check the rubber seal at the bottom of the door and the strips along the sides and top. Cracked or flattened seals let in water, pests, and hot Texas air. Replacement strips cost under $30 at any hardware store and install in minutes.

4. Tighten All the Hardware

The vibration of thousands of open/close cycles loosens nuts, bolts, and brackets over time. With a socket wrench, go around and snug up every fastener you can see, especially the bolts holding the track brackets to the wall.

5. Test Your Safety Sensors

Place a 2x4 flat on the floor in the path of the door and try to close it. The door should reverse immediately on contact. If it doesn't, the auto-reverse is malfunctioning and needs immediate attention, this is a safety requirement, not optional.

Pro tip: Schedule a professional tune-up once a year. Our techs catch issues you'd never notice, and it's far cheaper than an emergency repair call.

What Makes an Opener "Smart"?

A smart opener connects to your home's WiFi and pairs with an app. Key features include remote open/close, real-time alerts (e.g., "Your garage door has been open for 30 minutes"), activity history, and integration with smart home platforms like Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and Apple HomeKit.

How Austin's Heat & Humidity Affect Your Garage Door

April 22, 2026 Let's Roll Team 3 min read

Central Texas summers are brutal, triple-digit heat, intense UV, and humidity that swings dramatically. Your garage door feels every bit of it. Here's what Austin homeowners need to know to protect their investment through every season.

Heat Warps Wood Panels

Wooden garage doors and those with wood overlays are especially vulnerable to Texas summers. Prolonged heat exposure causes wood to warp, crack, and separate, especially on south- or west-facing garages with direct afternoon sun exposure. UV-resistant sealant applied annually can significantly extend panel life.

Metal Expands and Contracts

Steel and aluminum doors expand in heat and contract at night. Over years, this thermal cycling loosens fasteners, warps tracks slightly, and causes seals to degrade faster than in milder climates. Quarterly hardware checks are more important here than in most parts of the country.

South and west-facing garage doors in Austin receive the most sun exposure and need extra protection.

Rubber Seals Degrade Fast

The bottom seal and weatherstripping on your door are made of rubber, and rubber doesn't love Texas heat. UV exposure hardens and cracks seals over time, letting in hot air, water, and insects. Plan to replace seals every 2-3 years in Austin vs. every 5 years in cooler climates.

Humidity and Springs

Austin's humidity, especially during spring storm season, accelerates corrosion on uncoated torsion springs. If your springs aren't galvanized or powder-coated, consider upgrading to corrosion-resistant springs at next replacement. They cost slightly more but last significantly longer in humid Texas air.

Seasonal Maintenance Schedule for Austin

  • Spring (March-May): Inspect seals, lubricate all hardware, check spring tension before summer heat sets in.
  • Summer (June-Aug): Check opener heat sensors, verify door balance, inspect door finish for sun damage.
  • Fall (Sept-Nov): Replace any degraded seals, tighten hardware, test auto-reverse.
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Rare freezes can contract metal and crack brittle seals, inspect after any freeze event.

Garage Door Tune-Ups: Why Annual Maintenance Saves You Money and Keeps Your Door Safe

June 24, 2026 Let's Roll Team 5 min read

Your garage door is one of the hardest-working systems in your home. Most homeowners use it multiple times every day without giving it much thought, until something goes wrong. Whether it's a noisy door, worn rollers, broken springs, or an opener that suddenly stops working, many major garage door problems start as small issues that could have been prevented with routine maintenance.

At Let's Roll Garage Doors, we recommend scheduling a professional garage door tune-up at least once a year. Regular maintenance not only keeps your door operating safely and quietly, but it can also help you avoid costly repairs and extend the life of your entire garage door system.

What Is a Garage Door Tune-Up?

A garage door tune-up is a complete inspection and maintenance service designed to keep every moving part operating properly. During a professional tune-up, a trained technician carefully examines your garage door, opener, springs, cables, rollers, tracks, hinges, and safety features to identify potential issues before they become expensive repairs.

A typical garage door tune-up includes:

  • Full safety inspection
  • Garage door balance test
  • Spring and cable inspection
  • Roller and hinge inspection
  • Tightening loose nuts and bolts
  • Lubrication of all moving parts
  • Track inspection and adjustment
  • Opener performance check
  • Safety sensor alignment
  • Auto-reverse safety test

This preventative maintenance helps your garage door operate smoothly while reducing unnecessary wear on the entire system.

1. Improve the Safety of Your Garage Door

Garage doors can weigh anywhere from 150 to over 600 pounds depending on size and material. If springs, cables, or safety systems fail, serious injury or property damage can occur.

During a tune-up, we inspect broken or frayed cables, weak or worn torsion springs, loose hinges, bent tracks, faulty safety sensors, the auto-reverse function, and overall garage door balance. Making sure these components are operating properly helps protect your family, pets, vehicles, and belongings.

2. Prevent Expensive Repairs

Many homeowners wait until their garage door completely stops working before calling for service. Unfortunately, waiting often turns a simple repair into a much larger expense. An unbalanced garage door forces the opener to lift more weight than it was designed to handle, which shortens the lifespan of the motor and internal gears over time. Worn rollers can damage the tracks, loose hinges can crack door panels, and dry bearings increase friction that wears out moving parts much faster.

Routine maintenance catches these problems early, saving hundreds, or even thousands, of dollars in future repairs.

3. Extend the Life of Your Garage Door

Your garage door is an investment, and like any mechanical system, it lasts longer when properly maintained. Lubricating moving parts reduces friction, adjusting spring tension helps keep the door balanced, replacing worn rollers before they fail prevents additional damage, and tightening hardware keeps everything aligned. With annual maintenance, it's common for garage doors and openers to last several years longer than systems that receive little or no maintenance.

4. Keep Your Garage Door Running Quietly

A garage door should not squeak, grind, shake, or bang every time it opens. Noisy garage doors are usually caused by dry rollers, worn bearings, loose hardware, poor lubrication, or misaligned tracks. Most of these problems can be corrected during a routine tune-up before they become larger mechanical issues. A properly serviced garage door should open smoothly, quietly, and consistently.

5. Avoid Emergency Breakdowns

Few things are more frustrating than discovering your garage door won't open when you're trying to leave for work. Emergency repairs are often caused by components that have been wearing out for months. Routine inspections help identify weak springs, fraying lift cables, rollers near failure, loose brackets, opener problems, and safety sensor issues, letting us replace these parts before they fail and cause an unexpected breakdown.

6. Improve Garage Door Performance

Over time, garage doors naturally shift as homes settle and components wear. A tune-up ensures your door opens evenly, closes completely, travels smoothly, doesn't strain the opener, and seals properly against the floor. Proper adjustments improve overall performance while reducing wear on every moving part.

Why Annual Maintenance Matters

Think of your garage door like your vehicle, you wouldn't expect your car to last 200,000 miles without changing the oil. Your garage door works the same way. Small maintenance services performed once a year can dramatically reduce future repair costs while improving safety and reliability. Most garage door manufacturers also recommend regular maintenance to help maximize the lifespan of their products.

Signs Your Garage Door Needs a Tune-Up

If you notice any of these symptoms, it's time to schedule service:

  • Loud squeaking or grinding
  • Slow operation
  • Door feels heavy
  • Uneven movement
  • Garage door shakes while opening
  • Opener strains to lift the door
  • Rollers appear worn
  • Loose hinges
  • Door won't stay halfway open during a balance test
  • It's been more than one year since your last maintenance

Ignoring these warning signs often leads to larger repairs later.

Schedule Your Garage Door Tune-Up in Austin, TX

Whether your garage door is brand new or over 15 years old, routine maintenance can help extend its lifespan, improve performance, and prevent unexpected repairs. If your garage door has become noisy, isn't operating as smoothly as it should, or simply hasn't been serviced in over a year, now is the perfect time to schedule a professional tune-up.
